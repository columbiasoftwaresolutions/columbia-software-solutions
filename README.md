# Columbia Software Solutions (CSS)

Welcome to the team. This doc covers everything you need to get set up and start contributing.

---

## What we do

CSS is a student-run software club at Columbia where small teams build real software for real clients. No fake projects, no case studies. You work with an actual company or nonprofit, ship a real product in 8–10 weeks, and get mentored by an industry engineer through the whole thing.

Our first project is with **Refettorio Harlem**, a nonprofit restaurant in Harlem that serves 700+ free meals a week. We're building them a data management system — a WhatsApp bot for kitchen staff to log service data, a reporting dashboard, and eventually AI-generated grant reports.

## How we work

**Weekly sync** — 30 min, once a week per project team. Format: what did you ship, what are you working on, what's blocked. That's it.

**Pull requests** — every change goes through a PR. Your teammates review it, the mentor reviews it. This is how real teams work and it's how you'll get better fast.

**Code reviews** — our industry mentor does weekly code reviews. Take the feedback seriously, it's the most valuable part of being in CSS.

**Communication** — if you're blocked, say so in Slack immediately. Don't sit on a blocker for days. That's the one rule.

## Current project — Refettorio Harlem

**The problem:** Refettorio tracks meals served, food rescued, and community impact through spreadsheets and memory. This makes grant reporting painful and slow.

**What we're building:**

- **Phase 1:** A WhatsApp or SMS bot where kitchen staff can log each service in plain English ("tonight we served 87 sit-down meals, 23 takeaway, Baldor drop was 200 lbs") and AI parses it into structured data automatically. Mobile-first, dead simple.
- **Phase 2:** A reporting dashboard that auto-aggregates the data into weekly/monthly/YTD totals and lets staff query it in natural language.
- **Phase 3:** Auto-generated grant reports and social posts from the data.

**Their stack:** Funraise, Bloomerang, QuickBooks, Mailchimp, Squarespace, Instagram.

**Key constraint:** Non-technical staff using phones in a kitchen. If it requires training, it's too complex.

**Success looks like:** Staff log a service in under 3 minutes. Monthly grant report takes under 10 minutes to generate.
